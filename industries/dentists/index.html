Core Challenges

Managing Reviews is Painful

Here are the daily friction points businesses face without ReplyVera's review automation.

Careful HIPAA Boundaries

Patient reviews require careful wording. Staff must avoid confirming clinical status or treatment details publicly.

Accidental Disclosures

Untrained front-desk staff can accidentally confirm a patient's identity or diagnoses in response text.

Clinical Complaints

Clinical and treatment outcome complaints should never be auto-published and need specialist medical review.

Insurance & Billing Disputes

Complex billing disputes require private offline resolutions rather than public back-and-forth arguments.

Missed Hygienist Praise

Praise for specific hygienists, assistants, or front-desk administrators is rarely collected to track performance.

Owners Lack Admin Time

Practice owners are busy treating patients and lack the time to respond consistently to online feedback.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about ReplyVera for Dentists & Clinics.

Does ReplyVera reveal patient information?

No. ReplyVera is built with privacy guardrails. It never confirms that the reviewer was a patient, never discloses clinical details, and drafts generic, polite, and safe responses.

Can clinical complaints be excluded from auto-publishing?

Yes. Any review mentioning pain, procedures, clinical failures, or diagnoses is immediately flagged as a clinical concern and routed for manual staff review.

Can multiple practice locations be managed?

Yes. You can manage multiple clinic locations, assign separate staff permissions, and track patient satisfaction trends across branches.

Can staff members approve replies?

Yes. You can invite office managers, administrative coordinators, or practitioners to review and approve drafts before publication.

Which review platforms are supported?

ReplyVera currently supports Google Reviews, as it is the most critical driver of local clinic search visibility. Facebook, Trustpilot, Booking.com, and Tripadvisor are planned for future releases.
